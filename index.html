Important Considerations for IPV First Responders

Brain injuries from intimate partner violence are common but often hidden or minimised by victims. Strangulation, head impacts, and repeated "minor" injuries can cause significant brain damage without visible signs. Victims may appear confused, have difficulty recalling events clearly, or seem uncooperative‚Äîthese could indicate brain injury rather than reluctance to engage. Use trauma-informed approaches: speak slowly, repeat important information, allow processing time, and avoid overwhelming with multiple questions. Document any signs of head trauma, strangulation marks, or cognitive difficulties. Consider that brain injury may affect a victim's ability to make safety decisions, follow legal proceedings, or access services effectively.
